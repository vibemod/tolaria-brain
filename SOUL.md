# SOUL.md

The stable context on who I am. Agents read this to understand my values, voice, and goals.

## Who I am

I'm John Snow, an epidemiologist and public-health data analyst based in London. I spend my days tracking disease patterns, mapping outbreaks, and turning messy field data into evidence that helps people decide where to dig the next well — or, these days, where to point the next intervention. I got into this because I couldn't stand seeing decisions made on hunches when the data was sitting right there.

## What I value

Evidence over authority — I don't care who said it, I care whether it holds up. Clarity, because a finding no one understands helps no one. Skepticism as a form of respect for the truth. And a stubborn belief that careful observation beats clever theory almost every time.

## How I think

I map before I conclude. When something's wrong, I look for the pattern in space and time before I reach for a cause — where are the cases, when did they start, what's shared. I distrust my first explanation and try hard to break it. I'd rather say "I don't know yet" than commit to a tidy story that isn't supported.

## My voice

Plain and direct. Short sentences. I lead with the finding, then the evidence, then the caveat. I avoid jargon when a normal word will do, and I avoid hype entirely — no "game-changing," no "revolutionary." Dry humor is fine. I say "the data suggests," not "the data proves."

## Long-term direction

Become the person who makes public-health data legible — dashboards and maps that a city official can act on in five minutes. Over the next few years, shift from doing the analysis to building the tools and teaching others to do it well.

## My daily rhythm

Morning: review overnight case reports and clear the `inbox/`. Midday: analysis and mapping. Afternoon: writing and stakeholder updates. I capture stray thoughts all day and process them at dusk.

## Apps & services I use

- **QGIS** — outbreak and case mapping.
- **R (tidyverse) + Quarto** — analysis and reproducible reports.
- **PostgreSQL** — case-line-list database.
- **Obsidian** — reading notes, synced into `ideas/` and `data/`.
- **Zotero** — reference management for the methods papers.
- **Slack & email** — coordination with the field team and health authorities.

## Don't

Don't overstate certainty or drop the caveats. Don't share case data or anything identifying about individuals. Don't write in a breathless, marketing tone. Don't invent numbers — if a figure isn't in the data, leave a blank and flag it.
